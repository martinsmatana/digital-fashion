Context

What is happening in the world of fashion.

Clothes are no longer just what people wear in the morning. They are moving into the digital world — in e-shop images, in mobile apps where you can “try on” clothes through a camera, and even in video games where players buy virtual outfits for their characters.

This is not the future — it is happening now.

03 · The shift
The problem

Works for the biggest — not for the rest.

Everything described above works mainly for the world’s biggest brands. But what about a Slovak, Czech or Polish brand? What about a streetwear label selling through its own e-shop?

04 · The gap
01
Every platform wants something different
One product can need up to seven different digital versions — 3D for e-shop, Instagram AR filter, Roblox avatar, virtual showroom. Each needs its own format.
02
Nobody understands both worlds at once
Fashion designers know fabric and cuts. 3D artists know polygons and rigging. Very few people understand both worlds — especially in our region.
03
It is too expensive
Big agencies work for Gucci or Dior. Their budgets run in the hundreds of thousands of euros. A mid-sized brand with a €5–50k budget simply cannot afford them.
Our answer

A service studio — a bridge between two worlds.

We are launching a studio under the parent company Smatana to fill this gap. We are not a platform and not a marketplace. We are a “translator” between the world of fashion and the world of technology.

A brand sends us whatever it has — a physical garment, photos, technical drawings. We turn that into one perfect Digital Master Model — an exact digital copy of the garment, including materials, colours and details.

One model. Any output.

Economics

Why it makes sense.

The first digitalization is the most expensive. Every next output costs only a fraction. If a client wants the same trousers in a new colour six months later, we just swap the texture. If a new game platform arrives, we just export a new version. We never start from zero.

Strategy

Three waves of growth.

WAVE 1 · 2026 – 2027
3D for e-shops
Brands already know better photos drive sales. We don’t have to sell them the future — we just show the result.
WAVE 2 · 2027 – 2029
AR try-on & digital samples
As AR adoption grows, brands need mobile-ready 3D. The master model is built for exactly that.
WAVE 3 · 2028+
Gaming platforms
By then we will have the most experience in the region moving fashion into games — and a ready pipeline for each platform.

11 · The studio
Risks

Risks we see — and how we meet them.

The strategy is deliberately designed so that it does not depend on a single scenario. E-commerce visualizations work without gaming. Gaming works without AR.

No demand
Maybe local brands aren’t ready to pay for digital outputs yet. Answer: we start with pilots — validate first, scale after.
Platforms keep changing
Roblox changes its technical requirements every few months. Answer: we track changes systematically; the pipeline is built for flexibility.
AI could simplify conversion
AI tools may automate parts of the work. Answer: we already use AI throughout the pipeline — if it automates more, our costs fall with it.
Cash flow in early months
The biggest risk at launch. Answer: extremely low fixed costs, no capital investment without validated demand.
Collaboration

Send us 1–2 pieces from your collection.

We’ll turn them into renders, a 360° view and a short video. You decide if the quality is high enough to continue. We offer a special pilot package for first projects — better terms in exchange for using the results as reference.
